Canada Geese & Urban Development

How human landscapes reshape wildlife observation distribution in New Jersey.

Background

Canada geese can be found throughout North America, from the Arctic to northern Mexico. They are strongly associated with water, including marshes, lakes, and ponds, but they also feed in open grassy areas.

Because of this, they are often attracted to suburban lawns, parks, golf courses, and other developed spaces that provide both food and nearby water access.

Key idea: developed landscapes can unintentionally create ideal goose habitat.

Why Development Matters

Canada geese have increasingly established large populations in suburban and urban environments across the northeastern United States. Unlike migratory populations, these resident geese can thrive where habitat and food are consistently available year-round.

Suburban expansion often introduces maintained grass, retention ponds, fragmented wetlands, and edge habitat. Together, these features can support higher goose densities than more forested or less managed environments.

Key idea: development reshapes habitat rather than simply removing it.

Study Area

New Jersey is an ideal study area because it combines intense suburban development, dense transportation corridors, and strong variation in human population density across counties.

Counties in the northeastern part of the state, such as Hudson and Essex, are among the most densely populated, while southwestern counties remain much less dense.
