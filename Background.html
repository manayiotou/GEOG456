Canada Geese & Urban Development

How human landscapes reshape wildlife observation distribution in New Jersey

Background

Canada geese have increasingly established large resident populations in suburban and urban environments across the northeastern United States. Unlike migratory populations, these geese thrive in human-modified landscapes where food and habitat are consistently available.

Patterns of land development play a critical role in shaping these environments. Suburban expansion introduces large areas of maintained grass, stormwater retention ponds, and fragmented wetlands— all of which provide ideal conditions for geese. As a result, developed land can support higher densities of geese than more natural or forested landscapes.

New Jersey represents an ideal case study due to its extensive suburban development and rapidly changing land cover over recent decades. This project explores how land development patterns relate to the spatial distribution and density of Canada goose observations over time.
