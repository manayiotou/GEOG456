State Overview

From 2008 to 2023, New Jersey experiences steady expansion of developed land, especially along the I-95 corridor and major metropolitan regions. Forest and agricultural lands become increasingly fragmented, while geese populations concentrate near wetlands, reservoirs, and suburban open space.

Newark / Urban Core

Newark remains one of the most intensely developed areas in the state across all years. Land cover shows little structural change, but surrounding suburban expansion increases over time. Canada geese are primarily concentrated in parks, reservoirs, and industrial-adjacent green infrastructure.

Trenton Region

The Trenton area shows moderate but clear suburban growth between 2008 and 2023. Agricultural and semi-natural land is gradually converted into residential and commercial development, creating fragmented edge habitats that increase suitable environments for geese.

Coastal New Jersey

Along the Jersey Shore, development intensifies in tourism-driven towns and barrier island communities. While built environments expand, wetlands and coastal marshes remain important ecological zones that strongly influence geese distribution patterns.

Inland New Jersey

Inland regions experience the most visible land transformation, with suburban sprawl replacing forests and agricultural land over time. This creates a patchwork landscape of fragmented habitats and open spaces that strongly support adaptable species like Canada geese.
